# Extra Delight Legacy

Extra Delight Legacy is a Forge 1.12.2 port of the original Extra Delight mod for Farmer's Delight Legacy.

Extra Delight is a mod that vigorously expands upon farming and cooking in Farmer's Delight.

Oven - A 3x3 crafting grid that slowly bakes recipes as long as there is a heat source beneath and the appropriate bakeware in the tray slot. And if the heat source is a campfire it will put a neat little enclosure around it!

Dough Shaping Station - A stone cutter for dough! Shape pasta or cookies from a variety of doughs.

Mixing Bowl - Mix up all sorts of tasty treats! Load in the appropriate items (and perhaps fluids) and right-click on it with the appropriate utensil.

Mortar - Used to crush ingredients to get at the goodness inside - your first step towards bread made without straw! Will require its trusty partner the Pestle. Can also give fluids like Cooking Oil.

Drying Rack - Gotta get dry! Make snacks like jerky or dried fruit, or dry ingredients so they can be ground into powder.

Melting Pot - Melt solids into liquids. Requires a heat source underneath.

Chiller - Chill liquids into solids. Can create a variety of mainly chocolatey treats. Requires some sort of container for the fluids to set in. And having the drip tray emptied occasionally.

Vat (and Lid) - Ferment, cure, and pickle all the things! Can even do multi-stage processes.

Evaporator - Dry fluids into goodies. In this legacy build it currently dries water into salt when exposed to the sky in a warm enough biome.

Juicer - Squash fruits into tasty juices!

Spoon - You spin me right round, baby. Mix things up in the Mixing Bowl, scoop up tasty treats, or even peel ginger on the FD Cutting Board!

Pestle - The grind does not stop, and neither should you. Crush everything from wheat seeds, to roasted coffee beans, to dried agar agar sheets, or even flowers to get a bit more dye out of them. In the Mortar of course.

Bakeware/trays - For use in the oven (and the chiller occasionally too). A baking sheet, a muffin pan, a pie dish and more!

Grater - Totally shredded. Grate ginger, potatoes, carrots, and more on the FD Cutting Board.

Offset spatula - Your kitchen's fairy godmother! A wave of this magic wand and your kitchen is re-styled. Works on frosted gingerbread and chocolate blocks to change the patterns, as well as giving your Mortars, Mixing Bowls, Fermentations Vats, Lids, Evaporators, Fruit Bowls, and bakeware a new look.

Funnel - A hopper for fluids! Can drain in to the Mixing Bowl, out of the Mortar, and in and out of a Keg, Jar, or Chiller. Or you can manually tip fluid in the top using a bucket or Jar. It will even pick up a fluid block if one happens to be sitting on top of it and spit it out the bottom if there is space.

Jar - A means of picking up less than (and up to) a bucket of a fluid. For example, it can be used to scoop fluid out of the Mortar and transfer it to the Mixing Bowl, or just place it down on a shelf.

Keg - Stores up to 8 buckets of a single fluid. Best friends with the Funnel.

Tap - In its raw form. Right-click with a bucket to fill the bucket with water. Drippin.

Whisk - For getting those airy treats juuuuust right.

This legacy build includes the Extra Delight crop, wild crop, orchard, sapling, fruit-leaf, petal, and crop-processing chains that have been adapted for the local plant system.

Need some Vinegar in your life? How about some Breadcrumbs? Maybe some Frosting, or Cookie Dough? Condiments like Mayo or Ketchup? Cornmeal? Butter? Gravy? Whipped Cream?!

Suffice to say there is a lot to work with in Extra Delight, and even more things to make it into!

Many of the custom liquids have bucket or container workflows where the current legacy systems support them, including kitchen fluids such as juices, sauces, chocolate syrups, blood, tea, coffee, egg white, and custard.

Savory meals - Fried foods, salads, pasta, burgers, breakfast cereals.

Sweet, sweet desserts - Custards, ice creams, baked goods, popsicles!

Candies for after - All the chocolate you could ever want, mints, caramel.

Drinks to quench the deepest of thirsts - Juices, milkshakes, hot chocolate three ways, even eggnog.

Cookies for all you monsters out there - Shaped holiday cookies galore.

Snacks for when you just gotta munch on something - Chips, popcorn, pickled morsels.

Meaty delights - The legacy butchery content is local here, with knife-based butcher drops for organs, blood, fat, and other ingredients.

Feasts can grace your banquet table. Tuck into a hearty lasagna, dish yourself up some chili, slice up cheesecakes, dip any number of things into a chocolate fondue, or even sink into a whole r a i n b o w of jelly.

Or perhaps even have a cute picnic with a jug of something refreshing.

Two new wood sets! Cinnamon wood is a lighter, brighter orange than Acacia, while Fruit wood is a lighter, brighter sibling to Jungle. It even has the horizontal bark pattern.

Wooden decor and kitchen blocks are available for the supported vanilla, Cinnamon, Fruit, and optional compatible wood families implemented here, including Crimson, Warped, and Cherry families when their provider content is present.

### Wooden Stuff

Wallpaper - Comes in all 16 colours, and can have a wooden molding which can then be placed at the top or bottom of the block.

Spice Racks - Cute little shelves which have 4 inventory slots. They can be crafted in their filled variant to display some decorative jars, or unfilled to display the items stored.

Half Cabinets - Like the FD cabinets but only half as deep! Great for using as overhead cabinets, and can sneakily store just as much as their full-size siblings.

Knife Blocks - Visually store up to 4 supported knives.

Step Stools - A handy step up. Acts like a slab in that you will automatically step up onto it.

Countertop Cabinets - Look just like their regular FD cousins until you click the cogwheel and give it a block to use as a countertop texture, upon which it will sprout a little counter edge as well.

Sinks - A cabinet with a sink in it! For all your watery needs. Although it does have reduced storage capacity to make room for the plumbing. But it also has the option to set a countertop texture so it can fit right in with the rest of your benchtop.

Dried Corn Fences - Regular Fences not feel rustic enough for you? Try the Dried Corn variant to sprinkle in a little more visual interest.

Food Display - And if there is something you want to display without it being jumbled up in a bowl, look no further than the Food Display! Feel like opening a supermarket? You can even add a glass frontage by right-clicking on it with a glass block.

### Non-Wooden Stuff

Wreaths - Well they are wood-adjacent? Make a festive wreath out of supported leaf types and then display items on it.

Bows - The Wreaths' best friends! Come in all 16 wool colours and can be put on Wreaths, worn in your hair, or crafted into a pretty box.

Chocolate Boxes - They are boxes. You put chocolate or candy in them, then close the lid and give them to someone special.

Candy Bowl - Very similar in use, with 9 single-item slots for valid candies. Sure looks pretty on your counter though!

Chocolate building blocks - They are not quite full sets, but have blocks, stairs, slabs, doors, trapdoors, pillars, fences, and fence gates.

Cheese and Butter blocks! Blocks, slabs, and stairs.

Storage blocks - Crates and sacks for the Extra Delight crops and a few Vanilla things FD missed, like berries and mushrooms, and then sacks and bundles of some of the raw items like Cinnamon Bark, Corn Husks, and Flour. Stack your store rooms high!

Corn Husk Doll - A creepy lil doll made out of corn. Hang it from the ceiling if you really want.

Corn Cob Pipe - Bubbles!

Hanging foods - Ever wanted to hang your pantry from your ceiling? Extra Delight makes it possible! Available for onion, mint, ham, corn, garlic, two types of chili, and salami through the local curing chain.

Gingham block and carpets - Cute lil checkers to get that cozy kitchen feeling going. Available in all 16 colours, yes, even white.

Jar Display - Place various jars together, up to 4 to a block. Stock that pantry!

Flour, cornmeal, and breadcrumb floor sprinkles.

Fruit Bowl - A stylish bowl complete with a little pedestal to display your fruity wares.

Picnic Baskets - Comes in all the gingham options and can even be picked up while full of pickernick goodies. Who wants to go skip through the forest to visit Grandma?

A new banner pattern! - Two variants of a citrus slice to flap in the breeze.

Petals - Fruit tree leaves can drop petals on the ground below them as their flowering stages advance.
